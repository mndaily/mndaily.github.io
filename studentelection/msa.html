Where MSA Candidates Stand

E

lections for Minnesota Student Association, the University of Minnesota's undergraduate government, president and vice president will run from April 6 to April 8. Below are selected issues and candidates' positions. Most information was taken from their respective websites.

Click on a topic to see the candidates' positions. Click here to view their short bios.

Mental Health

Abeer Syedah
  • Specialized mental health resources for underserved populations
  • Expansion of student support groups
  • Ensure University offices make a commitment to improve mental health
Cam Holl
  • Student "De-Stress advocates" should be trained to allow students to talk about stress with their peers
  • A mental health task force that includes all organizations on campus should be formed
  • Campaign for a fall break
  • Expand mental health education in First Year Experience classes
  • Reduce wait times and expand appointment space at Boynton Health Services

Tuition/Costs

Abeer Syedah
  • Reject higher tuition for out-of-state and international students
  • Advocate for increased scholarship access
  • Encourage transparency regarding administrative budget
Cam Holl
  • Make the U-Pass universal
  • Build a lobbying plan that would focus on a tuition freeze
  • Work with departments and professors to cut costs in classrooms and find affordable textbooks
  • Restructure Student Services Fees to focus on student groups rather than administrative costs

Diversity/Inclusion

Abeer Syedah
  • Advocate for a center focused on religious, spiritual, and secular minorities
  • Increase demand of ethnic studies classes
  • Implement a "restorative justice model" for student conduct code violations, which entails holistically reviewing parties' needs
Cam Holl
  • The month of October should have a focus on campus inclusion
  • Partner with campus group to increase inclusion
  • Increasing access to facilities for meditation

Sustainability

Abeer Syedah
  • Create sustainability liasons in all residence halls and greek houses
  • Decrease waste produced by campus events
  • Incentivize use of reusable water bottles
Cam Holl
  • Connect MSA initiatives to each greek house residence hall to make sure students are aware of how they can be more environmentally consciousness
  • Promote an internal competition that each residence hall and greek chapters can compete in to find ways to improve sustainability.
